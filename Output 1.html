About the PPDO

The Provincial Planning and Development Office (PPDO) serves as the primary technical arm of the Provincial Governor on matters relating to overall socio-economic development planning and management. We are dedicated to translating the Provincial Government's vision into concrete, sustainable, and impactful programs for the welfare of all constituents.

Vision

"PPDO is a dynamic organization of competent staff with holistic and strategic thinking, managing development with integrity and professionalism "

Mission

"The PPDO is dedicated to: (1) Influence development partners in achieving results-oriented and people participated development planning process, and; (2) Formulate policy-based integrated and comprehensive development plans with the highest degree of excellence and teamwork."

Our Core Functions and Mandates

Comprehensive Planning

Formulate, integrate, and update the Provincial Development and Physical Framework Plan (PDPFP), Comprehensive Land Use Plan (CLUP), and various sectoral plans to guide long-term growth.

Research & Statistics

Gather, compile, and analyze socio-economic and physical data, providing essential statistical reports and evidence-based planning inputs for the Provincial Governor and other departments.

Investment Planning

Prepare the Provincial Development Investment Program (PDIP) and Annual Investment Program (AIP), translating long-term plans into specific, fundable projects and coordinating with the budget process.

Project Oversight (M&E)

Monitor the implementation of provincial programs and projects, conduct impact assessments, and evaluate performance to ensure efficiency and alignment with development goals.

Technical Support

Provide planning and technical assistance, data resources, and capability-building support to component Local Government Units (LGUs) to enhance local development planning.

Secretariat Services

Act as the Secretariat to the Provincial Development Council (PDC), Provincial School Board (PSB), and other inter-agency planning bodies, facilitating policy discussions and inter-departmental coordination.
